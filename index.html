charge-controller-lifepo

MSP430 based single cell LiFePo4 charge controller for photovoltaic applications; designed for low cost, robustness, easy soldering and hackability

Download this project as a .zip file Download this project as a tar.gz file

Welcome

The charge controller is made for the Opensource-solar.org project which is about designing open source hardware for pico photovoltaic systems.

The goal is to design a system for about 15 USD which can provide electricity for LED lighting, mobile phone charging and other small appliances which lasts at least 15 years.

Charge controller technical design goals

The charge controller has a 8 cell photovoltaics panel as its input, which delivers approximately 1 A at 4 V. This charges a LiFePo4 battery (starting at 3.2 V, 2.2 Ah up to bigger sizes with 10-20 Ah). The MSP430 microcontroller on the charge controller board prevents over charging and deep discharging by sampling the battery voltage. It also controls the two outputs which are a LED light and a DC/DC converter which delivers 5V @ 1A for charging mobile devices. The built-in capacitive touch peripherals of the microcontroller are used for switching the light and the charger on and off.
